# TaleMori Adventure Kit #1 Product Spec

## 1. Product Summary

**Working product name:** TaleMori Adventure Kit #1: Mori and the Lost Moonlight

**Product type:** A ready-to-use story adventure kit for screen-light parent-child play.

**Core idea:** Parents receive one gentle Mori story and a small set of guided activities that turn the story into a calm creative mission. The kit should feel easy to start, warm to share, and concrete enough that a parent understands what to do without planning from scratch.

**Pilot scope:** Start with a printable-only version. Use learnings from parent response before producing printed packs or collectible objects.

## 2. Target Audience

**Primary buyer:** Parents or caregivers of children ages 4-7 who want meaningful, low-prep activities at home.

**Primary child user:** A child ages 4-7 who enjoys stories, characters, drawing, pretend play, and small missions with an adult.

**Secondary users:**

- grandparents who want a simple activity to do with a child
- homeschool or enrichment parents looking for story-led activities
- gift buyers who want something warmer than a generic worksheet pack

## 3. Parent Problem Solved

Parents often want something calmer than a screen but easier than preparing a craft, lesson, or activity from scratch.

TaleMori solves this by giving parents:

- one story to read
- one clear mission to complete
- simple prompts for what to say next
- printable pages that use ordinary home materials
- a satisfying finish so the activity feels complete

The product should reduce planning, decision fatigue, and setup time.

## 4. Child Experience

The child does not feel like they are completing a worksheet. They feel like they are helping Mori.

Child-facing arc:

1. Mori has a gentle problem: a little moonlight is missing.
2. The child notices clues in the story.
3. The child makes or colors something useful for Mori.
4. The child chooses the next step in the mission.
5. The child completes the little light and earns a keepsake.

The activity should invite touching, coloring, choosing, retelling, pretending, and simple emotional language.

## 5. Product Promise

**Parent-facing promise:**

TaleMori helps you turn one gentle story into screen-light creative play with your child, without planning from scratch.

**Short promise:**

Read the story. Make the mission. Help Mori carry the little light home.

## 6. Emotional Theme

**Primary theme:** Gentle courage.

The kit should frame courage as one small next step, not fearlessness. Mori can feel unsure, ask for help, and keep going softly.

**Supporting themes:**

- noticing feelings
- trying one small step
- caring for a lost thing
- asking a trusted adult for help
- finishing a task with pride

Avoid heavy moral lessons. The emotional learning should be embedded in the story and prompts.

## 7. Proposed Kit Contents

Printable-only pilot:

- illustrated story booklet PDF
- printable mission page
- lantern-light coloring or cut-out page
- parent guide page
- completion badge or keepsake certificate
- simple material list

Printed mini kit later:

- small printed story booklet
- printed mission sheets
- badge or keepsake card
- parent guide card
- envelope or folder-style packaging

Optional future add-on:

- parent-supervised Mori collectible or lantern token

## 8. Story Booklet Scope

**Recommended length:** 12-16 pages including cover and end page.

**Reading time:** 5-8 minutes.

**Story structure:**

1. Mori notices the Lantern of Little Lights is dim.
2. A small moonlight piece is missing from the story forest.
3. Mori follows gentle clues: shimmer, shadow, leaf, path.
4. Mori feels unsure and pauses.
5. The child is invited to help Mori choose a next step.
6. Mori finds the lost moonlight hidden where someone needed comfort.
7. The lantern glows again.
8. Mori thanks the helper and names the brave beginning.

**Repeatable child phrase:**

"A little light for every brave beginning."

**Tone requirements:**

- gentle, wonder-filled, and specific
- no scary darkness
- no high-stakes danger
- no abstract instruction disguised as story

## 9. Printable Mission / Activity Scope

**Core activity:** Help Mori rebuild the missing little light.

Suggested printable pages:

- Moonlight path: a simple path with 3-5 story steps
- Little light pieces: color, cut, or circle the light Mori should carry
- Feeling clue: choose how Mori feels before the next step
- Lantern finish: draw or color the glow inside Mori's lantern

**Age fit:**

- Ages 4-5: coloring, circling, choosing between 2 options, retelling with parent support
- Ages 5-7: sequencing, drawing a clue, explaining a choice, writing one word if ready

Avoid too many pages. The pilot should feel complete with 2-4 activity sheets.

## 10. Parent Guide Scope

The parent guide should fit on 1-2 pages.

Include:

- 1-minute setup
- estimated time
- materials needed
- read-aloud prompt
- activity steps
- exact parent phrases
- adaptation for younger children
- extension for older children
- cleanup note

Example parent prompts:

- "What do you think Mori noticed first?"
- "Which little step should Mori try next?"
- "Mori feels unsure. What helps you when you feel unsure?"
- "I noticed you kept going even when the path was tricky."

Avoid making parents feel like teachers. The guide should feel like a calm companion.

## 11. Completion Badge / Keepsake Concept

**Concept:** Little Light Keeper Badge.

Format options:

- printable badge to color and cut
- small certificate: "I helped Mori carry the little light home"
- lantern card where the child draws the glow they made

Purpose:

- gives the activity a satisfying finish
- makes the kit feel more premium than loose worksheets
- creates a repeatable collectible system for later kits

Suggested badge text:

"Little Light Keeper"

## 12. Optional Collectible Concept

**Future object:** Mori lantern token or small Mori figure.

Pilot should not depend on the collectible. It can be shown as a future possibility only after safety, material, size, finish, and production checks.

Collectible directions:

- small lantern charm
- Mori standing figure with lantern
- flat story token that matches the printable badge
- kit-specific moonlight insert

Safety position:

These should be parent-supervised activity objects, not toys for toddlers or unsupervised small children.

## 13. Estimated Session Length

Target session:

- 5-8 minutes reading
- 10-15 minutes mission activity
- 2-5 minutes badge or retell

Total target:

- 17-28 minutes

Short version:

- read story and complete one mission page in 10-15 minutes

Extended version:

- add drawing, retelling, and badge display for 30-40 minutes

## 14. Suggested Materials Needed

Printable pilot:

- printer access
- plain paper
- crayons, colored pencils, or markers
- child-safe scissors if cutting is included
- glue or tape only for optional badge assembly

Keep the core mission usable without specialty craft supplies.

## 15. Suggested Format

### Printable-Only Pilot

Best first release because it is low-cost, fast to iterate, and simple to fulfill.

Recommended deliverable:

- one PDF package
- parent guide first
- story booklet
- mission sheets
- badge / keepsake page

Strengths:

- validates parent demand quickly
- avoids inventory risk
- easy to update after feedback
- works internationally

Risks:

- depends on parent willingness to print
- may feel less premium unless layout and illustration quality are strong

### Printed Mini Kit

Best second release if parents respond well to the pilot.

Recommended deliverable:

- printed story booklet
- printed activity cards or sheets
- keepsake badge card
- parent guide card
- small envelope or folder packaging

Strengths:

- feels more giftable
- reduces parent setup
- supports higher price

Risks:

- printing and packaging cost
- fulfillment complexity
- quality control
- inventory risk

## 16. Pricing Hypotheses

These are hypotheses, not final prices.

Printable-only pilot:

- low test price: USD 5-7
- stronger premium PDF price: USD 9-12
- bundle later: USD 19-29 for 3 printable adventures

Printed mini kit:

- pilot small pack: USD 14-19
- premium printed pack: USD 24-34 depending on printing, packaging, and included extras

Collectible add-on later:

- only price after safety, material, production, and fulfillment costs are known

Pricing test questions:

- Do parents value the story-led format enough to pay more than a generic printable?
- Does printed packaging significantly increase perceived value?
- Is the first purchase easier as a low-cost PDF or a giftable physical pack?

## 17. Risks / Open Questions

Product risks:

- The product may look beautiful but still feel unclear unless parents see actual sample pages.
- A printable-only product may feel less premium if the PDF layout is not polished.
- Too many activity pages could make the kit feel like homework.
- Too little activity could make the kit feel like only a storybook.
- Optional collectibles could distract from the first core product.

Parent clarity risks:

- Parents need to understand exactly what they receive before buying.
- The phrase "adventure kit" must be backed by concrete contents.
- The site should not imply curriculum, therapy, or guaranteed developmental outcomes.

Operational risks:

- Printed kit costs are unknown.
- Safety requirements for collectibles are not finalized.
- Fulfillment should not be added until the offer is validated.

Open questions:

- Should the first pilot be sold as a single PDF or offered free to the first testers?
- How many illustrated pages can be produced at launch quality?
- Should the activity use cut-outs or avoid scissors for easier parent setup?
- Should the first audience be local Indonesian parents, English-speaking parents, or both?
- Should pricing be in USD, IDR, or both?
