# TaleMori Kit 01 Story and Activity Plan

## 1. Story Premise

**Title:** Mori and the Lost Moonlight

Mori is the Little Lantern Keeper of the story forest. One evening, Mori notices that a tiny piece of moonlight is missing from the Lantern of Little Lights. The forest is not scary, but it feels softer and quieter than usual. Mori follows gentle clues with the child's help and discovers that the lost moonlight has gone to comfort a small sleeping seed that was afraid to begin growing.

The child helps Mori carry the little light back by choosing clues, coloring the lantern glow, and completing a simple moonlight path mission.

## 2. Emotional Theme

**Primary theme:** Gentle courage.

The story should show that courage can be small, quiet, and kind. Mori does not need to be fearless. Mori can pause, notice feelings, ask for help, and take one little step.

**Child-facing takeaway:**

"A little light for every brave beginning."

**Parent-facing takeaway:**

The activity gives parents a gentle way to talk about trying, pausing, and taking a next step without turning the moment into a lesson.

## 3. Page-by-Page Story Outline

This pilot story should be short enough for a 5-8 minute read-aloud. The first printable pilot can use 6-8 story pages, depending on illustration budget.

### Page 1: Mori Notices the Dim Lantern

**Story beat:** Mori is preparing the Lantern of Little Lights when one glow is missing.

**Suggested story text:**

Mori held up the Lantern of Little Lights.

One light twinkled.

One light hummed.

But one little light was gone.

**Parent prompt:**

"What do you notice in Mori's lantern?"

**Visual direction:**

Mori stands under soft trees, holding a lantern with one empty glow space.

### Page 2: A Soft Trail Appears

**Story beat:** Mori finds a tiny shimmer on a leaf and decides to follow it.

**Suggested story text:**

On a mossy leaf, Mori saw a tiny shimmer.

"Maybe the moonlight left a path," Mori whispered.

Mori took one small step.

**Parent prompt:**

"Can you point to the shimmer? Where should Mori look next?"

**Visual direction:**

Close view of a leaf, a small shimmer trail, and Mori kneeling gently.

### Page 3: Mori Feels Unsure

**Story beat:** The path becomes quiet. Mori pauses and notices a feeling.

**Suggested story text:**

The forest grew very quiet.

Mori's hands held the lantern tight.

"I feel a little unsure," Mori said.

Then Mori took a slow breath.

**Parent prompt:**

"What helps you when you feel unsure?"

**Visual direction:**

Mori paused at a forked path, lantern glowing softly, not dark or frightening.

### Page 4: The Child Helps Choose a Clue

**Story beat:** Mori finds three gentle clues and needs help choosing one.

**Suggested story text:**

Mori found three clues:

a silver feather,

a sleepy seed,

and a moon-shaped stone.

"Which one needs the little light?" asked Mori.

**Parent prompt:**

"Which clue would you choose? Why?"

**Visual direction:**

Three clear objects arranged on the forest floor for easy child pointing.

### Page 5: The Sleepy Seed

**Story beat:** Mori discovers the missing moonlight beside a tiny seed that is afraid to begin.

**Suggested story text:**

Beside the sleepy seed, the missing moonlight glowed.

"I wanted to grow," said the seed,

"but beginning felt too big."

Mori nodded. "We can begin small."

**Parent prompt:**

"What small step could help the seed begin?"

**Visual direction:**

Small seed tucked under a leaf blanket, moonlight nearby, Mori listening kindly.

### Page 6: Mori Shares One Little Light

**Story beat:** Mori helps the seed with one tiny glow and invites the child into the mission.

**Suggested story text:**

Mori lifted the lantern.

Not too bright.

Not too fast.

Just one little light.

"A little light for every brave beginning."

**Parent prompt:**

"Can you say Mori's words with me?"

**Visual direction:**

Lantern glow touching the seed gently; a small sprout shape may be visible.

### Page 7: The Path Home

**Story beat:** The moonlight returns to the lantern. Mori follows the path home.

**Suggested story text:**

The seed gave a tiny stretch.

The moonlight sparkled back into Mori's lantern.

Mori smiled.

"One small step was enough for tonight."

**Parent prompt:**

"What was Mori's small step?"

**Visual direction:**

Mori walking back on a clear moonlight path, lantern fuller than before.

### Page 8: Little Light Keeper

**Story beat:** Mori thanks the child-helper and names the keepsake.

**Suggested story text:**

Mori tucked the lantern close.

"Thank you, Little Light Keeper."

The forest glowed softly.

And one brave beginning had begun.

**Parent prompt:**

"What little light will you keep today?"

**Visual direction:**

Warm closing image of Mori facing the reader with lantern glow and badge motif.

## 4. Main Printable Activity Concept

**Activity title:** Help Mori Carry the Little Light Home

**Core mechanic:** The child completes a moonlight path by choosing, coloring, and marking 3-5 small story steps. The activity turns the story into a guided mission rather than a worksheet.

**Child actions:**

- color Mori's lantern glow
- choose one clue
- trace or mark the moonlight path
- draw one small brave beginning
- complete the Little Light Keeper Badge

**Parent role:**

- read the short prompts
- offer choices
- praise effort and next steps
- keep the activity calm and short

**Target activity time:** 10-15 minutes after reading.

## 5. Mission / Activity Page Concepts

Keep the pilot to 2-4 activity pages. The strongest first version is 3 pages plus the story booklet.

### Activity Page 1: Moonlight Path Mission

**Purpose:** Sequence the story and give the child a concrete mission path.

**Page elements:**

- simple path with 4 stops
- icons: leaf shimmer, quiet path, sleepy seed, lantern home
- check circles or color-in lights at each stop

**Child task:**

"Color one little light at each stop Mori finished."

**Parent prompt:**

"Which stop came first? Which one came last?"

### Activity Page 2: Choose Mori's Clue

**Purpose:** Give the child agency and a reason to talk.

**Page elements:**

- three clue illustrations: silver feather, sleepy seed, moon-shaped stone
- one large circle around the chosen clue
- small space for "Why did you choose it?"

**Child task:**

"Circle the clue you think Mori should follow."

**Parent prompt:**

"Tell me about your choice. There is no wrong answer."

### Activity Page 3: Lantern Glow Drawing

**Purpose:** Make the story emotionally personal without forcing disclosure.

**Page elements:**

- large lantern outline
- soft glow lines
- prompt area: "My little light can help me..."

**Child task:**

"Color the lantern glow. Draw or say one small step you can try."

**Parent prompt:**

"What is one little thing that helps you begin?"

### Activity Page 4: Little Light Keeper Badge

**Purpose:** Give the kit a satisfying finish and keepsake.

**Page elements:**

- printable badge or certificate
- Mori lantern icon
- child's name line
- phrase: "A little light for every brave beginning"

**Child task:**

"Color your badge and add your name."

**Parent prompt:**

"I noticed you helped Mori finish the mission."

## 6. Materials Needed

Core no-scissors version:

- printed pages
- crayons, colored pencils, or markers

Optional version:

- child-safe scissors
- glue stick or tape
- envelope or small folder for keeping the badge

Do not require specialty craft supplies for the pilot.

## 7. No-Scissors Version

The first pilot should work fully without scissors.

No-scissors approach:

- children color, circle, trace, check, and draw directly on the pages
- badge is a certificate-style page instead of a cut-out object
- lantern pieces are printed as "color the glow" shapes, not separate pieces
- parent guide says scissors are optional

This reduces setup friction and makes the kit easier for tired parents.

## 8. Optional Cut-Out Version Later

If parents want a more tactile version, add a later cut-out expansion.

Possible cut-out elements:

- small moonlight pieces to place on the path
- lantern glow insert
- Little Light Keeper badge
- clue cards

Safety and usability notes:

- keep pieces large
- avoid tiny tokens
- mark cutting as adult-assisted
- keep the no-scissors version as the default

## 9. Completion Badge Concept

**Badge name:** Little Light Keeper Badge

**Badge phrase:**

"I helped Mori carry the little light home."

**Secondary phrase:**

"A little light for every brave beginning."

**Format for pilot:**

- printable certificate or badge page
- no cutting required
- child colors Mori's lantern
- parent writes or child writes the child's name

**Future format:**

- die-cut badge card in printed mini kit
- sticker-style badge
- matching lantern token

## 12. Risks / Open Questions

Story risks:

- The story may become too quiet unless the mission has clear child participation.
- The missing moonlight should feel gentle, not scary.
- The seed character should not distract from Mori as the flagship.

Activity risks:

- Too many pages could feel like homework.
- Too few pages could feel thin for a paid product.
- Cut-outs may increase parent setup friction.
- Younger children may need more parent support than older children.

Production risks:

- Illustration consistency must be high for the kit to feel premium.
- Printable pages need enough contrast for home printers.
- PDF file size must stay practical.

Open questions:

- Should the story booklet be 6 pages for faster production or 8 pages for a more complete story arc?
- Should the badge be a certificate page or a cut-out badge in the first pilot?
- Should the first pilot include one sample page free on the website?
- Should the PDF be English-only first or bilingual later?
- Should parent prompts sit inside the story pages or only in the parent guide?
