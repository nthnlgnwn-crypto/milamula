# TaleMori Flagship Character Bible

## Purpose

TaleMori is moving from a generic story-kit site into a character-led story adventure kit brand. The main character needs to be ownable enough to carry books, printable activities, physical objects, parent trust, and repeat releases.

This document evaluates four possible flagship directions and compares them against the current placeholder characters: Milo, Nia, and Sami.

## Evaluation Lens

The strongest TaleMori character should:

- be memorable from the name and silhouette alone
- carry a repeatable story world, not only one story
- give children a simple emotional hook they can repeat
- give parents a trustworthy reason to buy
- translate into printable activities and parent-guided play
- work as a safe, parent-supervised 3D-printable collectible
- feel specific to TaleMori, not interchangeable with any children's brand

## Character Direction 1: Mori the Little Lantern Keeper

### One-Line Concept

Mori is a small lantern keeper who helps lost stories find their way home.

### Child-Facing Hook

"Mori lights the next little step."

### Parent-Facing Value

Mori gives families a gentle way to practice courage, patience, empathy, and problem solving through story-led play.

### Personality

- Warm, observant, and steady
- Curious, but not reckless
- Small enough to feel relatable to children
- Brave through tiny actions, not big speeches
- Notices when others feel lost, worried, or left out

### Emotional / Learning Theme

Gentle courage and emotional navigation. Children learn to notice feelings, name the next small step, ask for help, and care for others.

### Visual Silhouette

A small childlike keeper with a rounded hood, oversized lantern, simple satchel, and soft boots. The lantern creates an instant silhouette and a clear brand icon.

### Signature Object

The story lantern. It glows differently depending on the lesson: courage glow, kindness glow, patience glow, repair glow, wonder glow.

### 3D-Printable Collectible Potential

High. Mori can become:

- a small standing figure holding a lantern
- a separate lantern charm
- story tokens that fit into the lantern base
- a desk or shelf collectible for parent-supervised display
- a kit-specific lantern insert for each adventure

Safety note: small parts and lantern inserts should be treated as parent-supervised activity objects, not toys for toddlers.

### First Story Kit Title

Mori and the First Little Light

### First 3 Adventure Kit Concepts

1. Mori and the First Little Light
   - Theme: trying one small brave step
   - Story: Mori's lantern goes dim before the night path is finished, so Mori learns to ask for help and keep going gently.
2. Mori and the Missing Story Seed
   - Theme: patience and careful looking
   - Story: Mori follows tiny clues through TaleMori to help a story seed find the right place to grow.
3. Mori and the Bridge of Almost
   - Theme: trying again after frustration
   - Story: Mori cannot cross a wobbly paper bridge until each failed fold teaches one useful thing.

### Printable Activity Mechanic

"Light the next step" path cards. Children color or place lantern tokens on a short path, choose what Mori should try next, then retell the story in order.

### Why This Could Become Repeatable IP

Mori creates a clear repeat engine: every kit can introduce a new place in TaleMori, a new emotional challenge, and a new lantern glow. The lantern gives the brand a visual anchor, activity mechanic, collectible system, and repeated phrase. Mori can meet supporting characters later without losing the center of the brand.

### Risks / Weaknesses

- "Mori" may be close to the brand name, so the naming system must avoid confusion between TaleMori the world and Mori the character.
- Lantern imagery can become too bedtime-only if art direction is too dark or sleepy.
- Needs strong visual design to avoid looking like a generic forest child with a lamp.

## Character Direction 2: Tomo the Story Sprout

### One-Line Concept

Tomo is a tiny story sprout who grows new ideas when children read, make, and retell.

### Child-Facing Hook

"Tomo grows when stories are shared."

### Parent-Facing Value

Tomo makes early literacy, sequencing, and creative expression feel concrete for young children.

### Personality

- Cheerful, eager, and expressive
- Gets excited by new words and drawings
- Sometimes impatient to grow faster
- Learns that stories need care, time, and listening
- Friendly enough to act as a guide through activities

### Emotional / Learning Theme

Language growth, patience, sequencing, and creative confidence.

### Visual Silhouette

A small sprout character with two leaf ears, a rounded seed body, tiny feet, and a face that can show big feelings. The silhouette is soft and approachable, but less immediately distinctive than a lantern keeper.

### Signature Object

The story seed. Each kit includes a seed-shaped prompt, word card, or growth path.

### 3D-Printable Collectible Potential

Medium to high. Tomo can become:

- a simple sprout figurine
- stackable growth rings
- seed tokens
- a small planter-style display base
- interchangeable leaf expressions

The form is easy to print, but it may need extra design detail to feel premium instead of generic.

### First Story Kit Title

Tomo and the Tiny Story Seed

### First 3 Adventure Kit Concepts

1. Tomo and the Tiny Story Seed
   - Theme: words become ideas
   - Story: Tomo finds a silent seed and helps it grow by naming, drawing, and retelling.
2. Tomo and the Too-Fast Vine
   - Theme: patience and slowing down
   - Story: Tomo rushes a story vine until it tangles, then learns to pause and sequence one part at a time.
3. Tomo and the Sharing Garden
   - Theme: turn-taking and listening
   - Story: different story plants need different kinds of care, so Tomo learns to listen before helping.

### Printable Activity Mechanic

"Grow the story" sequencing pages. Children place beginning, middle, and end cards on a sprout path, then add drawing or word leaves.

### Why This Could Become Repeatable IP

Tomo directly supports the product promise: story plus activity. The growth metaphor works across reading, drawing, vocabulary, feelings, and habits. Tomo could be the best activity-guide mascot even if not the main flagship.

### Risks / Weaknesses

- Sprout characters are common in children's brands and may be less ownable.
- Parent value is clear, but child fantasy may feel softer than Mori or Piko.
- The visual silhouette could become too simple unless the design language is very specific.

## Character Direction 3: Piko the Pocket Fox

### One-Line Concept

Piko is a tiny pocket-sized fox who collects brave little ideas and turns them into adventures.

### Child-Facing Hook

"Piko has a pocket for every idea."

### Parent-Facing Value

Piko turns storytelling into sorting, choosing, pretending, and problem solving, which fits active children who need hands-on engagement.

### Personality

- Quick, playful, and inventive
- Loves small objects, clues, maps, and pockets
- Makes mistakes by rushing
- Learns to slow down, listen, and repair
- Brings energy without becoming chaotic

### Emotional / Learning Theme

Curiosity, self-regulation, flexible thinking, and repair after mistakes.

### Visual Silhouette

A compact fox with a large tail, tiny explorer vest, and oversized pocket. The tail and pocket make the silhouette easy to recognize.

### Signature Object

The pocket pouch. It holds story clues, feeling tokens, map scraps, tiny tools, or kindness notes.

### 3D-Printable Collectible Potential

High. Piko can become:

- a small fox figure with a visible pocket
- pocket tokens
- mini clue objects
- map markers
- swappable pouch inserts

The large tail and pocket create strong physical-product appeal, though print support and durability need careful design.

### First Story Kit Title

Piko and the Pocket of Brave Ideas

### First 3 Adventure Kit Concepts

1. Piko and the Pocket of Brave Ideas
   - Theme: choosing what to try next
   - Story: Piko's pocket fills with ideas, but only one small brave choice can open the path.
2. Piko and the Map That Moved
   - Theme: flexible thinking
   - Story: Piko learns that plans can change and still lead somewhere good.
3. Piko and the Oops Button
   - Theme: repair after mistakes
   - Story: Piko presses the wrong button and must use apology, listening, and a new plan to fix the adventure.

### Printable Activity Mechanic

"Fill the pocket" decision cards. Children cut or color idea tokens, place them in a printed pocket, choose one, and explain why.

### Why This Could Become Repeatable IP

Piko has strong child appeal, clear merchandising, and a natural kit mechanic. The pocket can hold a new object every release, making each kit feel collectible and repeatable.

### Risks / Weaknesses

- Fox characters are common, so Piko must have a highly specific TaleMori design language.
- Piko may skew more mischievous than trustworthy if not written carefully.
- Parent trust may be lower than Mori unless stories consistently show repair, listening, and guided choices.

## Character Direction 4: Lumi the Moon Rabbit

### One-Line Concept

Lumi is a gentle moon rabbit who helps children find calm, wonder, and one soft brave step before bedtime.

### Child-Facing Hook

"Lumi keeps a little moonlight for you."

### Parent-Facing Value

Lumi supports bedtime routines, emotional naming, calming rituals, and gentle courage.

### Personality

- Soft, quiet, and reassuring
- Notices small worries
- Speaks in simple, repeatable phrases
- Helps others breathe, pause, and try again
- More reflective than adventurous

### Emotional / Learning Theme

Calm, comfort, emotional regulation, and bedtime security.

### Visual Silhouette

A round rabbit with long ears, a moon satchel, and a crescent-shaped comfort object. The silhouette is cute and emotionally accessible.

### Signature Object

The moon button or moon pebble. It can become a calm-down prompt or bedtime ritual object.

### 3D-Printable Collectible Potential

Medium to high. Lumi can become:

- a seated moon rabbit figure
- moon tokens
- bedtime routine markers
- crescent display stand
- calm-breathing token

The collectible is appealing, but the brand could become too bedtime-specific.

### First Story Kit Title

Lumi and the Little Moon Button

### First 3 Adventure Kit Concepts

1. Lumi and the Little Moon Button
   - Theme: gentle courage at bedtime
   - Story: Lumi helps a child take one calm step when the room feels too big.
2. Lumi and the Cloud That Could Not Sleep
   - Theme: naming worries
   - Story: Lumi helps a worried cloud sort its busy thoughts into soft moon pockets.
3. Lumi and the Quiet Parade
   - Theme: respecting different energy levels
   - Story: Lumi learns that quiet children can still lead in their own way.

### Printable Activity Mechanic

"Moonlight calm cards." Children color moon phases, match them to feeling faces, then choose one calm action with an adult.

### Why This Could Become Repeatable IP

Lumi is strong for bedtime kits, comfort objects, and emotional regulation. The repeated moon motif could become visually recognizable and parent-trusted.

### Risks / Weaknesses

- Moon rabbit imagery is familiar and may be less ownable.
- Strong bedtime association may narrow the brand too early.
- Less natural fit for broad adventure kits unless paired with another flagship character.

## Placeholder Character Review

### Milo

Current read: gentle bedtime child character connected to the moon-button story.

Strengths:

- warm and emotionally safe
- parent-friendly bedtime tone
- easy to understand quickly

Weaknesses:

- common name and common bedtime/moon setup
- not visually ownable enough without the moon button
- feels like a one-book protagonist rather than a brand engine

### Nia

Current read: caring child character connected to pocket rain and sharing care.

Strengths:

- strong care and empathy theme
- useful for social-emotional stories
- can support parent values around kindness and sharing

Weaknesses:

- name and child silhouette are generic
- "pocket rain" is charming, but the character herself is not yet distinctive
- weaker collectible potential than object-led characters

### Sami

Current read: current Adventure Kit #1 visual placeholder and story-kit preview character.

Strengths:

- already tied to the current first kit assets
- simple and friendly
- can carry general adventure-kit messaging for now

Weaknesses:

- does not yet have a clear signature object, silhouette, or repeated phrase
- "Sami" does not immediately imply a world, mechanic, or collectible system
- likely to remain a placeholder unless rebuilt around a stronger symbol

## Recommendation

Replace Milo, Nia, and Sami as flagship candidates before rebranding the website.

The recommended flagship is Mori the Little Lantern Keeper.

Why Mori should lead:

- strongest fit with the TaleMori name and world
- clear signature object with story, visual, activity, and collectible uses
- high parent trust without feeling instructional or sterile
- strong enough to carry many kits through different lantern glows, places, and emotional themes
- easier to make ownable than a generic child, sprout, fox, or moon rabbit

Recommended character system:

- Mori: flagship character and brand anchor
- Piko: later supporting adventure character for high-energy kits
- Tomo: later activity-guide character for literacy, sequencing, and making prompts
- Lumi: later bedtime or calm-down sub-line character
- Milo, Nia, Sami: do not use as flagship names; optionally preserve them later as children Mori helps in individual stories, if the names still fit after the TaleMori world is defined

## First TaleMori Product Direction

Recommended first kit:

TaleMori Adventure Kit #1: Mori and the First Little Light

Core promise:

Read a gentle story, help Mori light the next small step, then make a printable lantern path together.

Repeatable child phrase:

"One little light, one little step."

Parent-facing promise:

A calm story-and-activity kit that helps children practice gentle courage through reading, choosing, coloring, and retelling.

First printable mechanics:

- lantern path coloring page
- feeling glow cards
- beginning-middle-end story path
- "what should Mori try next?" choice tokens
- parent prompt card with 3 simple read-aloud questions

First 3D-printable object direction:

- Mori figure with lantern
- separate lantern token
- three glow inserts: courage, kindness, try-again
- simple display base labeled "one little step"

## What Not To Do Yet

- Do not rebrand the website around Milo, Nia, or Sami.
- Do not rename the preorder kit until Mori's visual direction is approved.
- Do not overbuild a large cast before the flagship is proven.
- Do not make the 3D object feel like an unsupervised toy.
- Do not promise learning outcomes beyond parent-guided story, play, and practice.
- Do not make the first kit too lore-heavy. The first release should explain Mori in one sentence.

## Next Decision Checklist

Before changing the website, decide:

- final flagship name: Mori, or a close variant
- final repeated child phrase
- first kit title
- Mori's silhouette: hood, lantern, satchel, boots
- color palette for Mori and the lantern glow system
- whether Piko, Tomo, and Lumi appear now or stay hidden for later
- whether current Milo, Nia, and Sami assets are retired, archived, or reused only as temporary placeholders
