# TaleMori Kit 01 Content Draft

## 1. Final Kit Title

**TaleMori Adventure Kit #1: Mori and the Lost Moonlight**

## 2. Short Product Description

Mori and the Lost Moonlight is a ready-to-use story adventure kit for ages 4-7. Parents read one gentle Mori story, then guide a simple printable mission where children help Mori carry a little light home.

The kit is designed for screen-light parent-child play without planning from scratch.

## 3. 8-Page Story Booklet Manuscript

### Page 1: The Missing Glow

**Child-facing story text:**

Mori held up the Lantern of Little Lights.

One light twinkled.

One light hummed.

But one little glow was gone.

**Illustration direction:**

Mori stands in the story forest at dusk, wearing the moss-green hooded cape and holding the lantern. The lantern has several warm lights, with one small empty glow space. Keep the forest soft, safe, and wonder-filled.

### Page 2: The Leaf That Shimmered

**Child-facing story text:**

On a mossy leaf, Mori saw a tiny shimmer.

"Maybe the moonlight left a path," Mori whispered.

So Mori took one small step.

**Illustration direction:**

Close, intimate view of Mori kneeling beside a mossy leaf. A tiny trail of moonlight shimmer leads away from the leaf. The lantern gives a warm gold glow; the moonlight is pale silver.

### Page 3: The Quiet Path

**Child-facing story text:**

The path became very quiet.

Mori held the lantern close.

"I feel a little unsure," said Mori.

Mori breathed in.

Mori breathed out.

**Illustration direction:**

Mori pauses at a gentle fork in the forest path. The scene should not feel scary. Use soft shadows, warm lantern light, and rounded tree shapes. Mori's expression is thoughtful and tender.

### Page 4: Three Little Clues

**Child-facing story text:**

Then Mori found three little clues.

A silver feather.

A moon-shaped stone.

And a sleepy seed.

"Which one needs the light?" Mori wondered.

**Illustration direction:**

Show the three clues clearly on the forest floor so a child can point to them. Mori looks curious. The sleepy seed should feel small and gentle, not too character-heavy yet.

### Page 5: The Sleepy Seed

**Child-facing story text:**

Mori leaned close to the sleepy seed.

The missing moonlight glowed beside it.

"I wanted to grow," whispered the seed,

"but beginning felt too big."

**Illustration direction:**

The seed rests under a leaf blanket with the missing moonlight beside it. Mori listens carefully, holding the lantern low. The mood is comforting and still.

### Page 6: One Little Light

**Child-facing story text:**

Mori smiled softly.

"We can begin small."

Not too bright.

Not too fast.

Just one little light.

**Illustration direction:**

The lantern sends one gentle glow toward the seed. Show the light as soft and warm, like a small circle of courage. Mori should look calm and encouraging.

### Page 7: A Brave Beginning

**Child-facing story text:**

The seed gave a tiny stretch.

The moonlight sparkled back into Mori's lantern.

Mori whispered,

"A little light for every brave beginning."

**Illustration direction:**

A tiny sprout begins to appear from the seed. The missing moonlight returns to the lantern. This should be the emotional peak: warm, quiet, and satisfying.

### Page 8: Little Light Keeper

**Child-facing story text:**

Mori carried the lantern home.

The story forest glowed softly.

"Thank you, Little Light Keeper,"

Mori said.

"You helped the little light find its way."

**Illustration direction:**

Closing image of Mori facing the reader with the glowing lantern. Include a subtle badge or round emblem motif that connects to the Little Light Keeper Badge. The forest feels peaceful and complete.

## 4. Parent Guide

### Setup Time

**1 minute if printed already.**

Print the story pages and activity pages. Put out crayons, colored pencils, or markers.

### Materials Needed

Default no-scissors version:

- printed kit pages
- crayons, colored pencils, or markers
- a calm place to read and draw

Optional later version:

- child-safe scissors
- glue stick or tape

### How To Use The Kit

1. Read the story together.
2. Let your child point, notice, and answer if they want to.
3. Choose one activity path:
   - short version: Moonlight Path only
   - full version: Moonlight Path, Choose Mori's Clue, Lantern Glow, Badge
4. Keep the pace gentle. It is fine if your child talks more, draws more, or skips a prompt.
5. End with the Little Light Keeper Badge.

### Suggested Session Length

- Story: 5-8 minutes
- Activities: 10-15 minutes
- Closing badge: 2-5 minutes

Total: about 20-30 minutes.

### Parent Prompts / Questions

Use any of these. You do not need to use all of them.

- "What do you notice in Mori's lantern?"
- "Where do you think the shimmer goes?"
- "Mori feels a little unsure. What helps you when you feel unsure?"
- "Which clue should Mori follow?"
- "What small step helped the seed begin?"
- "What color should Mori's little light be?"
- "What is one little thing you can try today?"

### Gentle Parent Language

Use effort-focused phrases:

- "I noticed you kept going."
- "You helped Mori choose a next step."
- "That was a gentle idea."
- "You made the lantern glow in your own way."

Avoid pressure:

- Do not force a child to share a personal fear.
- Do not correct the child's drawing unless they ask for help.
- Do not turn the activity into a lesson or quiz.

### Gentle Closing Ritual

Hold up the finished badge or lantern page and say:

"Mori says thank you, Little Light Keeper."

Then invite the child to repeat:

"A little light for every brave beginning."

Optional final question:

"Where should we keep your little light today?"

## 5. Activity Pages Copy

### Activity Page 1: Moonlight Path Activity

**Page title:** Help Mori Follow the Moonlight Path

**Child instructions:**

Mori followed the little shimmer.

Color one light at each stop.

Can you help Mori remember the way?

**Path stops:**

1. The empty lantern
2. The shimmering leaf
3. The quiet path
4. The sleepy seed
5. The lantern home

**Parent prompt:**

"Which stop came first? Which stop came last?"

**No-scissors design notes:**

Use a printed path with five lantern circles to color. No pieces need to be cut out.

### Activity Page 2: Choose Mori's Clue Activity

**Page title:** Which Clue Needs the Light?

**Child instructions:**

Mori found three little clues.

Circle one clue for Mori to follow.

There is no wrong answer.

**Clue labels:**

- silver feather
- moon-shaped stone
- sleepy seed

**Parent prompt:**

"Tell me about your choice."

**Optional extension for older children:**

"Draw one more clue Mori could find."

**No-scissors design notes:**

Use large clue illustrations with enough white space for circling or coloring.

### Activity Page 3: Lantern Glow Drawing Activity

**Page title:** Make Mori's Lantern Glow

**Child instructions:**

Color Mori's little light.

Draw the glow inside the lantern.

Then finish this sentence with words or a picture:

My little light can help me begin by:

**Parent prompt:**

"What is one small step you can try?"

**For younger children:**

They can simply color the lantern and tell the parent their answer.

**For older children:**

They can draw or write one word, such as "try," "ask," "breathe," "draw," or "share."

**No-scissors design notes:**

Use one large lantern outline. Do not require assembly.

## 6. Little Light Keeper Badge Copy

**Badge text:**

Little Light Keeper

**Parent line:**

"I noticed you helped Mori finish the mission."

## 7. Notes For Future Indonesian / Bilingual Version

The first version can launch in English, but the concept should preserve room for an Indonesian or bilingual edition.

Translation notes:

- Keep sentences short.
- Preserve the repeated phrase rhythm.
- Avoid overly formal Indonesian.
- Choose warm parent-child language, not school worksheet language.
- Test whether "Little Light Keeper" should become a direct translation or a more natural Indonesian phrase.

Possible Indonesian phrase directions to test later:

- "Satu cahaya kecil untuk setiap awal yang berani."
- "Cahaya kecil untuk langkah berani."
- "Penjaga Cahaya Kecil" for Little Light Keeper.

Open bilingual format options:

- English story with Indonesian parent guide
- bilingual story text on each page
- separate English and Indonesian PDFs

The first pilot should not solve all language versions at once.

## 8. Notes For PDF Layout / Design

Recommended structure:

1. Cover
2. Parent quick-start page
3. Story pages 1-8
4. Activity Page 1: Moonlight Path
5. Activity Page 2: Choose Mori's Clue
6. Activity Page 3: Lantern Glow
7. Little Light Keeper Badge
8. Optional feedback page or QR code later

Design direction:

- premium storybook feel
- moss green, parchment cream, lantern gold, moonlight silver
- large readable type for story pages
- parent guide in simple blocks
- activity pages with enough white space for children
- no cluttered worksheets
- no dense instruction panels

Story page layout:

- one main illustration per page
- 2-5 short lines of text
- generous margins
- optional small parent prompt at the bottom or in the guide only

Activity page layout:

- clear title
- one child instruction
- one parent prompt
- large drawing/coloring area
- minimal icons

Print notes:

- test in color and grayscale
- keep activity line art clear
- avoid backgrounds that waste too much ink
- keep file size practical for download

## 9. Risks / Open Questions

Story risks:

- The seed may become too prominent and compete with Mori if illustrated too expressively.
- The story may feel too quiet unless the activity adds clear child participation.
- "Lost moonlight" must feel gentle, not scary or sad.

Activity risks:

- The sentence prompt on the lantern page may be too advanced for some 4-year-olds; drawing or spoken answers must be acceptable.
- Too many pages could feel like homework. The pilot should stay at three activity pages plus the badge.
- The badge may feel less special if it is only a certificate page; visual design must make it feel like a keepsake.

Product risks:

- Parents may want to see sample pages before joining or buying.
- The first paid version needs enough illustration quality to feel worth paying for.
- A no-scissors version is easier, but a later tactile cut-out version may feel more magical.

Open questions:

- Should parent prompts appear directly on story pages, or only in the guide?
- Should the first product preview show all activity pages or only one sample?
- Should the badge be landscape, portrait, or circular badge-style within a page?
- Should the story booklet be formatted for home printing as A4, US Letter, or both?
- Should the pilot be English-only, Indonesian-only, or bilingual after testing?
